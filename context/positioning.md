# Positioning

## One-liner
**"System design explained like you're 5 — by a working engineer."**
The simplest system design content on Instagram that still gets you through your interview.

## The open lane (from competitors.md)
- ByteByteGo owns *rigorous + polished* (513k). Meme accounts own *funny + shallow*. Nobody owns *ELI5 + trustworthy* for interview preppers. That's the wedge.
- Differentiators: (1) every concept gets one everyday analogy, (2) fixed recognizable visual template, (3) ends with the interview relevance beat, (4) authored by a named working engineer (bio credibility, faceless delivery).

## Brand
- Account name pattern: keyword-searchable, e.g. "Karthik | System Design" in the name field (personal-brand goal requires his name attached; exact handle TBD at launch — pick one that's available across IG/TikTok/YouTube for cross-posting).
- Bio: who it's for + what they get + credibility. E.g. "System design, minus the jargon · New reel 4×/week · Built by an engineer who does this for a living."
- Everything in English, globally accessible examples (Netflix, Uber, WhatsApp).

## What this channel is NOT
- Not a DSA/LeetCode account (crowded; different pillar).
- Not deep-dive rigor (ByteByteGo already won that).
- Not trend/meme-first entertainment.
- Not (yet) a funnel to a paid product — CTA priority is follow/save/send, building the personal brand.
