# Content pillars & topic universe

Three pillars (algorithm needs 2–3 consistent categories to classify the account):

## Pillar 1 — "How X actually works" (reach engine, ~50% of posts)
ELI5 breakdowns of famous systems. Highest viral potential — everyone uses these apps.
Topics: How Netflix streams to 200M people without crashing · How WhatsApp delivers a message in <1s · How Google returns results in 0.4s · How Instagram stores billions of photos · How Uber matches you to a driver · How Ticketmaster dies when Taylor Swift tours (and how it shouldn't) · How URL shorteners work · How Spotify recommends songs · How banks never lose your money (transactions) · Why your food delivery map updates live.

## Pillar 2 — Core concepts via analogy (authority engine, ~35%)
One building block per reel, one everyday analogy each.
Topics (ranked by interview frequency, 2026): load balancing (restaurant host) · caching + invalidation/stampede (memorized answers) · database indexing (book index) · sharding & consistent hashing (library branches) · replication & CAP/PACELC (photocopies) · message queues, Kafka vs SQS (post office) · CDN (local warehouses) · rate limiting (bouncer) · API gateway (hotel front desk) · SQL vs NoSQL · WebSockets vs polling · microservices vs monolith · idempotency · vector databases & RAG (2026 interviews now expect AI components — differentiator few reels cover).

## Pillar 3 — Interview tactics (conversion engine, ~15%)
The "follow me" reason for preppers.
Topics: the 4-step framework to answer any design question · requirements-clarifying questions that impress · back-of-envelope estimation in 30s · trade-off phrases interviewers love · red flags that fail candidates · "Design Twitter" in 60 seconds · what interviewers actually grade (2026: cost awareness, multi-region, AI/RAG components, sharper trade-offs).

## Rules
- Every reel = exactly one idea. If a script needs "and also", split it.
- ELI5 always, but end Pillar 1–2 reels with one interview-relevant beat.
- Recycle every reel's diagram into a carousel (near-zero cost, education carousels over-perform).
- Backlog target: keep 20+ scripted topics ahead at all times. The ~40 topics above ≈ 10 weeks; rotate formats (X vs Y, myth-busting, "explain in 30s") to extend to 156 posts.
