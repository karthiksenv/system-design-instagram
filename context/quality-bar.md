# Quality bar

## Reference creators (operator-confirmed blend)
- **ByteByteGo (Alex Xu)** — visual clarity of diagrams; every frame should be screenshot-worthy.
- **Fireship** — pacing, wit, zero wasted seconds; personality despite technical content.
- **NeetCode** — calm methodical trust; "this person actually knows" energy.
The target voice: *ByteByteGo's diagrams, Fireship's pacing, NeetCode's trustworthiness — at an ELI5 level.*

## A reel ships only if:
1. Hook text ≤8 words and creates a curiosity gap in frame 1.
2. Works fully muted (captions + visuals carry it).
3. One concept only; a smart 15-year-old would get it (ELI5 bar).
4. Technically defensible: simplified, never wrong. (Working-engineer reputation is the moat — a viral-but-wrong reel damages the personal-brand goal.)
5. Something moves on screen at least every 2 seconds.
6. ≤60s unless the topic truly earns more.
7. Matches the fixed visual template (colors/font/style).
8. Ends with a save/send CTA.

## Explicitly avoided
- Slow intros ("hey guys, today we'll...").
- Over-production — 2026 algorithm favors clear over polished.
- Jargon without an analogy in the same breath.
- Factually hyped claims (e.g., wrong numbers about Netflix scale) — simplification yes, fabrication no.

## Tone
Confident, friendly, lightly witty (Fireship-lite). No cringe forced memes; humor comes from analogies, not sound effects.
