# Audience

## Primary audience (operator-confirmed)
**Global/US-centric interview preppers**: CS students in final year + recent grads + junior engineers worldwide preparing for SDE interviews at product companies/FAANG. English-first content.

## Profile
- Age ~20–27. Consumes tech content on Instagram, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, LinkedIn.
- Pain points: system design rounds feel unbounded and scary; textbooks (DDIA) too slow; paid courses ($50–$400: Grokking, ByteByteGo, HelloInterview) are expensive; wants compressed, visual, confidence-building explanations.
- Jobs-to-be-done: (1) understand a concept in 60 seconds, (2) feel prepared, (3) save content for pre-interview review, (4) look smart sharing it with peers.
- Behavior (2026 research): education audiences on IG **save and share heavily but comment rarely**; education is among the strongest niches (~2.1% median ER; education brands ~5.4%; micro-accounts <10k often hit 5–8%).
- Watches ~50% of video muted → on-screen text/captions mandatory.

## Content-depth decision (operator-confirmed)
**ELI5 / beginner-friendly.** Maximize reach; simplify aggressively even if senior engineers nitpick. Analogies-first (restaurant = load balancer, library index = database index). Note the tension: the audience is interview preppers, so ELI5 explanations should still end with the "why it matters in your interview" beat where natural.

## Secondary audiences (don't optimize for, don't alienate)
- Indian interview-prep audience (largest raw IG tech population — will show up regardless; English content captures them).
- Curious undergrads not yet interviewing (future preppers).
