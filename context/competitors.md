# Competitor landscape (researched July 2026)

## Direct — system design on Instagram
- **@bytebytego (Alex Xu)** — ~513k IG followers; 1M+ newsletter subs. Polished animated architecture diagrams, interview-canonical. The category king. Weakness: assumes baseline knowledge, corporate tone, not ELI5.
- **Level Up Coding (Nikki Siapno)** — system design newsletter (tens of thousands of subs) with strong visual-diagram social presence.
- **System Design One / Neo Kim** — newsletter-first, visual system design breakdowns cross-posted to social.

## Adjacent — tech education on IG (format competitors)
- **@tom.developer** — ~392k, developer perspective tech content.
- **@madeline.m.zhang** — ~127k, daily AI & coding concepts, senior big-tech engineer.
- **@allthecode (Simon Barker)** — ~82k, software dev content.
- **@c_language_basics** — ~62k, C/C++/DSA tips (shows even narrow niches sustain 50k+).
- Numerous DSA/interview-prep meme-and-tips accounts in the 10k–500k range, heavily India-skewed.

## YouTube/newsletter players who could enter IG anytime
ByteByteGo (already in), NeetCode, Gaurav Sen, Exponent, HelloInterview, Jordan Has No Life.

## Gap / opportunity
No dominant account does **ELI5, analogy-first system design reels** for interview preppers. ByteByteGo owns "rigorous + polished"; meme accounts own "funny + shallow". The open lane: *"system design explained like you're 5, that still gets you through the interview"* — simple analogies, one concept per reel, consistent visual identity.

## What the winners do (patterns to copy)
- One concept per post; instantly recognizable visual template.
- Diagrams animate progressively (nothing static on screen >2s).
- Captions restate the concept + CTA to save/share.
- Cross-post identical content to YouTube Shorts/TikTok/LinkedIn for free extra distribution.
