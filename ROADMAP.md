# Roadmap

Nine months, four reels a week, 50,000 followers. This is the plan I'm
holding myself to, and the points at which I've agreed in advance to change
course rather than argue with the data.

Targets come from `context/success-metrics.md`. Topics come from
`context/content-pillars.md`.

## Phase 1 — Prove the format (months 1–2)

Target: 300–1,000 followers by the end of month 1.

Nothing here is about follower count. It's about whether the format works
at all and whether I can actually sustain the cadence on nights and
weekends.

- Register the handle across Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok so cross-posting
  stays possible later
- Lock the visual template — colours, font, diagram style. Once it's set it
  doesn't change, because recognition is the whole point of consistency
- Ship the first 8 reels from Pillar 1, the "how X actually works" topics,
  since those have the widest possible audience
- Establish the Sunday batch: 4 scripts, 4 diagram builds, one session
- Confirm the free AI-voice tier covers roughly 12 minutes of narration a
  month. If it doesn't, that's a blocker to solve now, not in month 4

Judge this phase on 3-second hold rate, not followers. Below 40% means the
hooks are wrong and everything downstream is wasted effort.

## Phase 2 — Find what breaks out (months 3–4)

Target: 2,000–4,000 by the end of month 3, and at least one reel past
100,000 views.

- Rotate all three pillars so the algorithm has clean categories to work with
- Start recycling each reel's diagram into a carousel — near-zero extra work,
  and education carousels tend to out-engage reels
- Every fortnight: rank reels by sends-per-reach, not likes. Sends are
  weighted several times heavier, and they're the honest signal that someone
  found it useful enough to forward
- The moment anything crosses 250,000 views, make three more in that exact
  sub-format within the week

If month 3 closes under 1,500, the visual format changes. Not the niche —
niches need longer than that to compound.

## Phase 3 — Compound (months 5–7)

Target: 6,000–12,000 by month 5, 15,000–25,000 by month 7.

- Format experimentation should be finished. This phase is about volume of
  attempts at a known-good format
- Move deeper into Pillar 3, the interview tactics, since that's what
  converts a viewer into a follower rather than a passing view
- Start the case-study topics — design Instagram, WhatsApp, Uber, payments —
  which reward the vocabulary built in earlier months
- Reply to every comment and DM for the first hour after posting

Under 5,000 at month 5 is the point for a bigger swing: a carousel-heavy
mix, collaborations with adjacent accounts, or repositioning from ELI5
toward interview-answer-focused.

## Phase 4 — Push or report honestly (months 8–9)

Target: 50,000.

- Lead with whatever the data says works, not with what's most interesting
  to make
- Two to four genuine breakouts are required to land this. They aren't fully
  within my control, which is the honest weakness of the whole plan
- Write the post-mortem in month 9 whether or not the number lands. A public
  account of what worked is worth more to me than the number itself

## What doesn't move

Four reels a week. One idea per reel. Technical correctness — a viral reel
that's wrong costs more than a mediocre one that's right, because the entire
point is credibility.
